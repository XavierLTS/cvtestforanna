I am a final-year undergraduate in the Business of the Creative Industries at the University of York, working across screen production, event coordination, cultural programming, visual content and film research.

My focus is screen producing — connecting creative ideas with the practical structure that delivers them, and keeping audience experience in view at every stage. I am most useful in the space between a director's intention and the workflow, communication and scheduling that carry a project from brief to screening.

I am particularly drawn to Chinese and Asian screen cultures, cross-cultural storytelling and international audience engagement — how stories are made legible, circulated and received as they move between contexts. My work consistently returns to one question: how does creative work reach, and resonate with, the people it is made for?

Thinking, in practice.

FILM / RECEPTION STUDY

Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon × Shadow

A comparative study of two landmarks of Chinese-language cinema — examining cultural legibility, audience reception, paratexts, subtitling and the circulation infrastructure that carries a film across international contexts. The project asks how meaning survives translation, and what is gained or lost as a story travels to new audiences.
